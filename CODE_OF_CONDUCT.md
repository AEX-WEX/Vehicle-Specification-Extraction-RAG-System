# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassing-free experience for all community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, experience level, nationality, personal appearance, political beliefs, race, religion, sexual identity, sexual orientation, or socioeconomic status.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that creates a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what benefits the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying
- Insulting or derogatory comments
- Personal attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Other conduct considered inappropriate in a professional environment

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Project documentation and wikis
- Community events and meetings
- Project communication channels
- Interactions related to the project in other spaces

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing community standards.

### Reporting

To report unacceptable behavior, contact: `conduct@example.com`

Please include:
- Description of the behavior
- Context and who was involved
- When it occurred
- Any relevant communication

All reports are confidential.

### Response

Maintainers will:

1. **Investigate** the report promptly
2. **Contact** the reported person for their perspective
3. **Determine** appropriate action
4. **Notify** both parties of the decision
5. **Implement** the decided action

### Consequences

Consequences for unacceptable behavior may include:

- Warning and education
- Temporary restriction from participation
- Permanent removal from the community
- Escalation to relevant authorities if necessary

## Appeal Process

If you disagree with an enforcement action, you may appeal to: `appeals@example.com`

Include:
- Details of the incident
- Why you believe the action was unjust
- What outcome you believe is appropriate
